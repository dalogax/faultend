Data Processing Agreement

Last updated: May 2026 · Incorporated by reference into the Terms of Service

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is entered into between Faultend ("Processor") and the user of the Faultend service ("Controller"). It applies whenever the Controller routes HTTP traffic containing personal data of third parties through a Faultend fault server, and is required by GDPR Article 28.

By using Faultend you agree to this DPA. No separate signature is required.

1. Definitions

"Personal data", "data subject", "processing", "controller", and "processor" have the meanings given in GDPR Article 4. "Services" means the Faultend proxy and traffic inspection platform available at faultend.com.

2. Roles and responsibilities

The Controller determines the purposes and means of processing — including which traffic to route through Faultend, whether recording is enabled, and what rules are configured. Faultend acts solely as Processor and processes personal data only on the Controller's documented instructions.

3. Subject matter and nature of processing

Faultend receives HTTP requests originating from the Controller's application and, depending on the configured rules, forwards them to one or more backend URLs and/or returns mock responses. When recording is enabled, Faultend stores a copy of each request and response for the Controller's inspection. The following technical measures apply to all stored traffic:

  • Authentication and credential headers (Authorization, Cookie, Proxy-Authorization, X-API-Key, X-Auth-Token) are replaced with [redacted] before being written to the database.
  • Stored traffic logs are automatically deleted after 7 days.
  • Recording can be disabled entirely per fault server.

4. Categories of personal data and data subjects

The categories of personal data and data subjects processed depend entirely on the HTTP traffic the Controller routes through Faultend. Typical examples in a development and testing context include identifiers (user IDs, email addresses in paths or bodies) and behavioural data in API responses. The Controller is solely responsible for ensuring the categories of data routed are consistent with the intended use described in Section 6.

5. Duration

This DPA applies for the duration of the Controller's use of the Services. Traffic logs are retained for a maximum of 7 days and then automatically deleted. Upon account deletion, all remaining data is permanently erased within 30 days.

6. Permitted purposes and instructions

Faultend processes personal data only for the purpose of providing the Services as configured by the Controller. The Controller's instructions are expressed through the fault server configuration (routing rules, recording settings, headers to preserve). Faultend will promptly inform the Controller if, in its opinion, an instruction infringes applicable data protection law.

7. Processor obligations

Faultend agrees to:

  • Process personal data only on the Controller's documented instructions.
  • Ensure that persons authorised to process the data are bound by appropriate confidentiality obligations.
  • Implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures, including encrypted transit (HTTPS), httpOnly and Secure session cookies, and access-controlled database infrastructure.
  • Notify the Controller without undue delay and within 72 hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting data processed under this DPA.
  • Assist the Controller in fulfilling its obligations to respond to data subject requests, to the extent possible given the nature of the processing.
  • Delete or return all personal data upon termination of the Services, at the Controller's choice, unless storage is required by applicable law.
  • Make available to the Controller all information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with GDPR Article 28.

9. Controller obligations

The Controller represents and warrants that:

  • It has a lawful basis under applicable data protection law for routing any personal data through Faultend.
  • It will not route payment card data (PCI DSS scope), protected health information (HIPAA scope), or special-category data under GDPR Article 9 without Faultend's prior written consent.
  • Where its end-users' personal data will be processed, it has provided appropriate transparency to those individuals about the use of Faultend as a development proxy.
  • It will prefer non-production, anonymised, or synthetic data for testing where feasible.

10. Data subject rights

Faultend will assist the Controller in responding to data subject requests to the extent technically possible. The Controller can delete all traffic logs for a server instantly from the server settings panel. For other assistance, contact dalogax@gmail.com.

11. International transfers

Faultend stores and processes all data within the European Union. Should any transfer outside the EEA become necessary, Faultend will ensure appropriate safeguards are in place (e.g. Standard Contractual Clauses) before the transfer occurs.

12. Governing law

This DPA is governed by the laws of the European Union and, where applicable, Spanish law. Disputes are subject to the jurisdiction of the courts of Spain, without prejudice to the Controller's right to bring proceedings before the courts of its place of establishment.
