Data Processing Agreement

Last updated: May 2026 · Incorporated by reference into the Terms of Service

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is between you ("Controller") and Daniel Lopez Garcia operating Faultend ("Processor"). It governs the processing of personal data under GDPR Art. 28 whenever you route traffic that contains personal data of third parties (your end-users) through Faultend fault servers.

By using Faultend you accept this DPA. No separate signature is required.

1. Roles

You are the data controller. You determine the purposes and means of processing (what traffic you route, whether recording is enabled, what rules you set).

Faultend is the data processor. We process personal data only on your documented instructions — namely, to proxy and (optionally) log the HTTP traffic you direct through your fault server.

2. Subject matter and nature of processing

Faultend receives HTTP requests from your application and forwards them to the backend URL(s) specified in your rules. When recording is enabled, it stores a copy of each request and response in our database for debugging and inspection purposes. Authentication headers are redacted before storage (Authorization, Cookie, Proxy-Authorization, X-API-Key, X-Auth-Token).

3. Categories of data and data subjects

The categories of personal data and data subjects depend entirely on the traffic you route. Common examples include:

  • End-user identifiers (user IDs, email addresses in request paths or bodies)
  • API authentication tokens (partially redacted per §2)
  • Any personal data contained in JSON request or response bodies

You are responsible for ensuring you have a lawful basis to route this data through a third-party processor. Do not route payment card data (PCI DSS), protected health information (HIPAA), or special-category data (GDPR Art. 9) without our prior written agreement.

4. Duration

This DPA applies for the duration of your use of Faultend. Traffic logs are retained for 7 days and then automatically deleted. On account deletion, all logs and configuration data are permanently erased within 30 days.

5. Processor obligations

We commit to:

  • Process personal data only on your instructions (the rules and recording settings you configure).
  • Ensure that staff with access to personal data are bound by confidentiality obligations.
  • Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures (HTTPS in transit, encrypted database at rest, access controls).
  • Notify you within 72 hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting your data.
  • Delete your data upon account termination or on request.
  • Make available information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with GDPR Art. 28.

7. Your obligations

As controller you are responsible for:

  • Having a lawful basis under GDPR (or equivalent applicable law) for routing personal data through Faultend.
  • Providing transparent information to your end-users about the use of Faultend as a test proxy where required.
  • Not routing data that requires additional agreements (PHI, PCI, special-category data) without our prior consent.
  • Disabling recording or using Faultend only with non-production / anonymised data when processing real personal data is not necessary for your test.

8. Data subject rights

We will assist you in responding to data subject requests relating to data stored in traffic logs. You can delete all logs for a server instantly from the settings panel. For other requests, contact dalogax@gmail.com.
