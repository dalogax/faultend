1. Who we are

Faultend (faultend.com) is operated by Daniel Lopez Garcia. Contact for data protection matters: dalogax@gmail.com.

We act as data controller for the personal data of our registered users. When users route their own application traffic through Faultend, we act as a data processor on their behalf — see the Data Processing Agreement.

2. Data we collect and why

2.1 Account data

When you sign in via Google or GitHub OAuth we receive and store your email address, display name, avatar URL, and the OAuth provider identifier. This is required to create and maintain your account.

Legal basis: performance of a contract (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)).

2.2 Session data

We issue a session cookie (faultend.sid) to keep you authenticated across page loads. The session record is stored in our database and expires after 30 days of inactivity.

Legal basis: legitimate interest in providing a secure, stateful service (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f)); strictly necessary — exempt from consent under ePrivacy Directive Recital 25.

2.3 Traffic logs (proxy data)

When you use a Faultend fault server, every HTTP request and response passing through it is captured and stored. This includes request method, path, headers, body (up to 10 MB), response status, response body, and timing. Sensitive headers (Authorization, Cookie, Proxy-Authorization, X-API-Key, X-Auth-Token) are redacted before storage and replaced with [redacted].

Traffic logs are deleted automatically after 7 days. You can also delete them manually at any time from the server settings. Recording can be disabled entirely per server.

Legal basis: performance of a contract (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)); the logging is the core feature you signed up for.

Important: Traffic logs may contain personal data of your application's end-users. You are the data controller for that data; we are your data processor. Please review our DPA and our Terms of Service before routing production traffic through Faultend.

2.4 Analytics (optional, consent-gated)

With your permission, we use PostHog (EU region, eu.i.posthog.com) to collect usage events (e.g., sign-up, sign-in) and anonymised page-view data. We send PostHog your internal user ID; email and name are only shared if you explicitly consent to analytics.

Legal basis: consent (GDPR Art. 6(1)(a)). You can withdraw consent at any time via the cookie preferences link in the footer.

5. Your rights

Under GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • Rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate data.
  • Erasure — ask us to delete your account and all associated data (account deletion is available in your profile settings; traffic logs delete automatically after 7 days).
  • Portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Restriction — ask us to restrict processing while a dispute is resolved.
  • Objection — object to processing based on legitimate interest.
  • Withdraw consent — withdraw analytics consent at any time via cookie preferences.

To exercise any right, contact dalogax@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Spanish data protection authority (AEPD) or the supervisory authority in your country of residence.
